# Maven-Toys-Data-Analysis (Interactive Dashboard using Power BI)

## Project Overview
This project presents an end-to-end sales and inventory analysis of Maven Toys using Power BI. The dashboard provides insights into revenue, profit, order trends, product performance, and store-level efficiency across 2022 and 2023. It highlights key business drivers, identifies underperforming areas, and uncovers shifts in customer demand to support data-driven decision-making.

## Project Objective
To conduct a comprehensive analysis of Maven Toys’ retail performance by examining sales, profit, and inventory data across stores and product categories. The project aims to uncover trends, identify performance gaps, and deliver actionable recommendations that support revenue growth, profitability improvement, and operational efficiency..

## Key Insights

- There is a significant category shift, with Arts & Crafts surpassing Toys as the most ordered category as seen in the previous year. This reflects evolving customer preferences and presents an opportunity to realign inventory and merchandising strategies. Scaling supply and visibility for Arts & Crafts, while strategically managing Toys inventory, will be key to maximizing revenue and minimizing excess stock risk.
 
- Toys are both the most frequently out-of-stock category and a primary revenue driver, highlighting a critical supply gap. Addressing stock availability in this category presents a strong opportunity for revenue growth.

- Etch a Sketch is a major revenue driver in the bottom 5 performing stores (Out of stock in La Paz 1) and has a low stock (24) across the five stores. This could could affect revenue and should be attended to. 
  
- Campeche 2 store is the lowest-performing store and currently holds low stock levels of three top-selling products. This stock gap may be a key factor contributing to its poor performance.

- Morelia 1 store has consistently maintained a high profit margin, driven by strong performance from Barel O’Slime, Colorbuds, and Jenga. Replicating this product mix across other stores could help improve overall profitability.

## Final Conclusion/recommendation

- Ciudad de Mexico (Stores 1–4) accounts for over 11% of total revenue, driven by a group of top-performing products—Lego Bricks, Color Buds, Action Figures, and Rubik’s Cube. This suggests a highly effective product mix, merchandising, and marketing strategy. Replicating these success factors across similar stores could significantly enhance performance and drive incremental revenue growth.

- The five lowest-performing stores—La Paz 1, Campeche 2, Durango 1, Cuernavaca 1, and Toluca 2—generate a significant portion of their revenue from a limited set of products, including Nerf Gun, Lego Bricks, and Animal Figures. These high-performing items can be leveraged to cross-sell lower-performing products such as Play-Doh Toolkit, Play-Doh Can, and Toy Boxes to improve overall store performance (e.g e.g., "Buy Nerf gun, get Play-Doh 20% off").

- Play-Doh Toolkit underperforms in revenue generation while holding significant inventory (1,387 units), highlighting a clear case of overstocking. This excess inventory is likely locking in working capital that could be redeployed to more profitable, fast-moving products.
